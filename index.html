accidentsHappen

A car accident story inspired us to use a dataset to analyze and predict the chances of getting in an accident based on specific criteria and data. We see great potential in utilizing this dataset to provide interesting insights and research. By entering certain criteria such as zip codes, seasonal trends, severity, and traffic attributes, one can better forecast the chances of getting into a car accident. It is a simple and easy tool that any driver can use.

Seasons throughout 4 Years

Throughout the four years, we were hoping to find certain trends but then the months of one year. Thus, we can see there are certain trends, especially during the summer, that might lead to higher accident rates.

Weekend vs Weekdays

Based on the assumption that a normal work week is Monday to Friday and weekend is Saturday to Sunday, we can surmise that a higher percentage of accidents occur within the weekday, even though there are five days in a week day and only two days on a weekend.

Time of the Day

We wanted to group the time in parts of the day since we can accurately dig insights onto which parts of the days have more data. we came up with time slots that are divided in such way:

Early Morning (3 am to 6:59 am)
Morning (7 am to 10:59 am),
Afternoon (11 am to 2:59 pm),
Late Afternoon (3 pm to 6:59 pm),
Night (7 pm to 10:59 pm),
Late Night (11 pm to 2:59 am)
